# Provenarc Aero Solutions - Expedited Service Policy

**Effective Date:** March 4, 2026  
**Policy:** Selective Expedited Service for Aviation Sales Professionals

---

## STANDARD POLICY: METHODICAL EXECUTION

**Default Position:** We operate on a quality-first timeline with standard turnarounds of 10-40 business days depending on service tier. Our 3:1 post-production ratio ensures insurance-grade deliverables that withstand rigorous scrutiny.

**Standard Turnaround Times:**
- Tier 1 (1 field day): 10-15 business days
- Tier 2 (2 field days): 15-20 business days
- Tier 3 (3 field days): 20-25 business days
- Tier 4 (5 field days): 25-30 business days
- Tier 5 (7 field days): 30-40 business days

**Messaging:** "We're thorough, not fast. Your aircraft deserves documentation done right, not done quickly."

---

## EXPEDITED SERVICE EXCEPTION

### Who Qualifies for Expedited Service

**Aviation Sales Professionals Only:**
- Aircraft brokers and sales agents
- Brokerage firms (Mason Amelia, Jetcraft, Avpro, etc.)
- Dealer networks
- MRO facilities offering sales support
- Corporate flight departments managing pre-sale transitions

**Why Brokers/Agents Get Priority:**
- Transaction deadlines are non-negotiable (buyers walk, deals collapse)
- Professional documentation accelerates closings and protects commissions
- Repeat business potential (brokers sell multiple aircraft annually)
- Volume partnership opportunities
- Industry reputation and referral network value

**Individual Aircraft Owners:**
- Generally NOT eligible for expedited service
- Exception: Pre-sale situations with documented buyer deadline and broker involvement
- Must justify business case for expedited timeline

## OPERATIONAL REQUIREMENTS FOR EXPEDITED SERVICE

### What Expedited Service Requires

**Immediate Field Deployment:**
- 48-72 hour mobilization (vs. 30-60 day standard booking)
- Priority scheduling (bump non-expedited clients if necessary)
- Weekend/evening field work if needed

**Dedicated Post-Production Team:**
- Assigned processor (not shared queue)
- Extended work hours (evenings/weekends)
- Compressed QC cycles (still rigorous, but accelerated review)
- Real-time client communication

**Resource Allocation:**
- Pull staff from other projects
- Overtime labor costs
- Opportunity cost (delaying other clients)

**This is why the premium is justified and necessary.**

## QUALIFICATION & APPROVAL PROCESS

### Step 1: Eligibility Verification
- Client must be licensed broker, agent, dealer, or MRO sales support
- Provide broker license number or company affiliation
- Individual owners must demonstrate broker involvement and transaction urgency

### Step 2: Business Case Review
- Documented transaction timeline (LOI, purchase agreement, closing date)
- Buyer's pre-buy inspection deadline
- Risk assessment (will deal collapse without expedited service?)

### Step 3: Operational Feasibility
- Can we deliver on expedited timeline without compromising quality?
- Do we have staff/equipment availability?
- Is aircraft accessible within 48-72 hours?

### Step 4: Premium Selection & Approval
- Standard Expedited (+25%): Field manager approval
- Priority Expedited (+40%): Co-managing member approval

### Step 5: Deposit & Scheduling
- Full 50% deposit required upfront (non-refundable for expedited)
- Field service scheduled within 48-72 hours
- Deliverable deadline confirmed in writing

## POSITIONING: PREMIUM FOR URGENCY, NOT SPEED

### Key Messaging

**"We're thorough, not fast—but we understand transaction deadlines."**

Expedited service is NOT about cutting corners. It's about:
- **Resource reallocation:** We pull staff from other projects to prioritize yours
- **Extended hours:** Evening and weekend work to compress timelines
- **Opportunity cost:** We delay other clients to serve you immediately

**The premium compensates us for disruption, not shortcuts.**

Quality remains non-negotiable:
- Same OCR verification standards
- Same multi-stage QC review
- Same insurance-grade deliverables
- Just faster resource allocation and processing

**Analogy:** Fine art restoration doesn't get "rushed"—but a museum can pay a premium to bump the queue for a special exhibit opening. Same quality, different timeline.
