Our Process

From initial consultation to final delivery, here's how Provenarc Autos and Classics preserves your vehicle's complete provenance and condition baseline

The Provenarc Autos & Classics Workflow

1

Initial Consultation

We begin with a detailed discussion of your vehicle, documentation needs, and timeline. During this consultation we identify your vehicle type and historical significance, current documentation status and any gaps in provenance, specific concerns such as upcoming auctions or pending sales, restoration or modification documentation requirements, and desired service tier including 3D scanning needs.

You receive a detailed proposal with transparent pricing, estimated timeline, and scope of work. All questions are answered before you commit.

2

Service Booking & Deposit

Once you approve the proposal, we reserve your field service date with a fifty percent deposit. This deposit covers initial logistics, travel procurement, and equipment preparation. Most clients book thirty to sixty days in advance for optimal scheduling, though expedited service is available for urgent needs through our dealer and auction house partner program.

3

Pre-Service Coordination

Before field service, we confirm vehicle location and accessibility, verify adequate workspace near the vehicle or in your collection facility, coordinate access for both indoor and outdoor documentation if needed, arrange power availability for scanning equipment, and schedule owner or representative availability for document handover and return.

For vehicles in restoration or undergoing work, we coordinate with the shop to ensure safe access without disrupting their operations.

4

Field Service Mobilization

Our technician travels to your vehicle location worldwide. We arrive with portable scanning equipment, 3D scanning systems for comprehensive documentation, protective document handling supplies, and secure data storage for immediate backup.

Travel expenses are billed at actual cost with no markup. You receive itemized receipts for transparency.

5

On-Site Documentation

All work is performed at your vehicle location. You maintain physical custody of original documents throughout the entire process.

Provenance Documentation:

We scan original build sheets and factory documentation, complete service and maintenance records, restoration documentation including photos and receipts, ownership transfer records and titles, modification and upgrade documentation, awards and concours history, registry certifications and correspondence, and any other documentation that contributes to your vehicle's provenance story. High-resolution scanning with immediate quality verification ensures nothing is missed.

3D Condition Documentation:

We capture exterior documentation of bodywork, paint, and trim details, interior documentation of upholstery, dashboard, and original components, undercarriage documentation of frame, suspension, and exhaust, engine bay documentation of motor, accessories, and detailing, and mechanical systems documentation of critical components and unique features. This creates a comprehensive digital record of your vehicle's physical condition.

Restoration Progress Documentation:

For vehicles undergoing restoration, we document before, during, and after conditions with both traditional photography and 3D scanning. This comprehensive record proves the scope and quality of restoration work performed.

6

Immediate Document Return

All original documents are returned to you before our technician departs your location. You sign a completion checklist confirming all originals have been returned and accounted for. Digital files are uploaded to secure cloud storage as backup.

Your vehicle's provenance documentation never leaves your property. Zero shipping risk, ever.

7

Post-Production Processing

After field service, our team performs comprehensive post-production work at our facility. This includes optical character recognition processing and verification for all scanned documents, metadata tagging and chronological organization for easy navigation, point cloud processing and optimization for 3D scans, quality control review and verification at multiple stages, and collector-grade deliverable preparation in multiple formats.

We maintain a three to one post-production ratio, meaning three days of processing for every one day of field service. This methodical approach ensures collector-grade deliverables that meet auction house and registry standards.

8

Deliverable Preparation

Your final deliverables are prepared in professional, accessible formats. You receive complete digital archives organized chronologically and by document type, searchable PDF files with OCR-enabled text for easy research, 3D model files in industry-standard formats for sharing with restorers or appraisers, web-based 3D viewer access for remote sharing with potential buyers, and comprehensive provenance reports suitable for authentication and registry submissions.

9

Cloud Hosting & Delivery

All documentation is uploaded to our secure RecordVault platform with lifetime cloud hosting included, redundant backup across multiple data centers, granular access controls for sharing with buyers, auction houses, or authentication experts, and mobile-friendly access from any device worldwide.

Physical deliverables are also provided via archival-quality USB drive and optional hard drive for local backup and permanent storage.

10

Final Invoice & Support

The remaining fifty percent balance is due upon delivery of final deliverables. Invoice includes itemized travel expense reconciliation with receipts.

Ongoing support is available for questions, additional access provisioning, or future documentation updates as your vehicle's story continues.

Why We Don't Rush

Provenarc Autos and Classics operates on a methodical, quality-first timeline. Collector-grade documentation requires comprehensive quality control that cannot be compressed without compromising deliverables. Our three to one post-production ratio ensures proper OCR verification, metadata accuracy, and multi-stage review.

We position ourselves as thorough, not fast. Your vehicle deserves documentation done right, not done quickly. Collectors value accuracy over velocity, and our reputation is built on flawless execution that meets auction house and registry standards.

Restoration Documentation Protocol

For vehicles undergoing restoration, we offer comprehensive before, during, and after documentation that proves the scope and quality of work performed.

Pre-Restoration Baseline

We document the vehicle's condition before restoration begins. This baseline includes complete photographic documentation, 3D scanning of the entire vehicle for precise measurements, documentation of all original components and numbers-matching parts, and detailed condition assessment for restoration planning.

Progress Documentation

During restoration, we can schedule periodic documentation visits to capture disassembly stages, fabrication and repair work in progress, component refurbishment and replacement, paint and bodywork stages, and reassembly with detail work. This creates a comprehensive visual record of the restoration process.

Completion Documentation

After restoration, we document the finished vehicle with the same comprehensive approach used for the baseline. This includes complete 3D scanning for comparison with pre-restoration condition, detailed photography of all restored components, documentation of work performed with receipts and records, and professional deliverables suitable for registry submissions and authentication.

Value and Authentication

Comprehensive restoration documentation supports higher valuations by proving the scope and quality of work. Registry organizations and authentication experts value our detailed records when evaluating vehicles for certification. Potential buyers gain confidence from complete visual documentation of the restoration process.

Registry Submission Support

Many collector vehicles are eligible for marque-specific registry certification. Our documentation packages are designed to support these submissions with professionally organized provenance records and condition documentation.

What Registries Require

Registry organizations typically require comprehensive documentation of ownership history and transfers, verification of numbers-matching components and originality, photographic evidence of condition and configuration, service and maintenance records proving proper care, and documentation of any modifications or restoration work.

How We Support Your Submission

Our deliverables are organized specifically to meet registry requirements. You receive chronologically organized ownership and service records, detailed photography and 3D documentation of all critical components, professionally formatted reports suitable for submission, and ongoing support for any clarification questions from registry officials.
