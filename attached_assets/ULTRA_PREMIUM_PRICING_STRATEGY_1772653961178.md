## PRICING PHILOSOPHY SHIFT

### The Realization:
You're not competing with $5K records digitization services. You're competing with:
- **Pre-buy inspections:** $25K-$75K for jets
- **Major maintenance events:** $100K-$500K+
- **Insurance premiums:** $50K-$200K+ annually

On a $20M-$50M aircraft, a $100K-$150K comprehensive documentation package is:
- **0.5% of aircraft value** (rounding error)
- **Less than one month's depreciation**
- **Less than annual insurance premium**
- **Insurance against 6-7 figure losses** from incomplete records

## PSYCHOLOGY OF SIX-FIGURE PRICING

### Why $150K-$250K Works for High-Value Jets

**1. Anchoring to Aircraft Value:**
- $150K is 0.75% of a $20M jet
- $250K is 0.5% of a $50M jet
- **Framing:** "Less than 1% of your aircraft's value to protect 100% of its provenance"

**2. Comparison to Alternative Costs:**
- Pre-buy inspection: $50K-$75K (doesn't include documentation or 3D)
- Major maintenance event: $200K-$500K (routine, expected)
- **Framing:** "Comprehensive documentation costs less than a single engine overhaul"

**3. Risk Mitigation Narrative:**
- Lost records = $500K-$2M in value loss
- Incomplete records delay sale 90-120 days = $150K-$300K in holding costs
- **Framing:** "One-time investment prevents seven-figure losses"

**4. Status & Exclusivity:**
- Six-figure service = ultra-premium positioning
- Not accessible to piston/turboprop owners
- **Framing:** "This is the documentation standard for the world's finest aircraft"

**5. Insurance Integration:**
- Annual insurance: $100K-$300K on high-value jets
- Baseline documentation can reduce premiums 5-10%
- **Framing:** "Pays for itself in insurance savings over 3-5 years"
